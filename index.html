About

Matthias “Matt” Beitl is a front-end web craftsman who started building websites when Netscape was in its prime; his first website went online in 1998. When Microsoft added XmlHttpRequest to Internet Explorer, Matt began creating applications for browsers, at a time when “web applications” were nothing but a concept on paper – years before the term “AJAX” was coined. Since then he has been working on web-related projects spare-, part- or full-time.

I specialize in responsive high performance websites and the CSS trickery that comes with them.

Today Matt indulges HTML5 and CSS3, with sprinkles of ECMAScript 5 (both vanilla javascript and frameworks such as jQuery). And he enjoys it more than ever. According to him, …

… thanks to Google Chrome, tools for web developers have never been better.
